EvalLense01 · Foundation
01 · Brand & Foundation

One lens, two worlds.

EvalLense focuses the chaos of incoming pitch decks into a single, sourced, comparable signal — the way a camera lens resolves scattered light into one sharp image. The brand lives in two registers that share one palette.

Brand world — cinematic
Dark, neon, dimensional. The unicorn (the startup) and the lens. Used for hero, big moments, statements.
Signature — the lens & the score
An exploded lens resolves into one score (8.7). The literal metaphor of the product.
Design principles
  • Lens clarity. Reduce noise to one clear, sourced signal. Whitespace and quiet structure over decoration.
  • AI prepares, humans decide. Every interface keeps the final call in human hands; AI output is advisory.
  • Evidence over opinion. Show what was found, where it came from, what is missing — never a bare score.
  • Subtle layering. Craft is invisible. Soft, violet-tinted depth; hairline borders; whisper-quiet elevation.
  • One accent, with meaning. Violet leads action; the lens gradient marks the product's signal; color always means something.
  • Calm motion. Deceleration easing, no bounce. Scroll-driven reveals that follow the reader.

EvalLense08 · Layout & application
08 · Layout & Application

Patterns, and how to ship them.

A few repeatable arrangements cover most screens. Max content width 1180–1200px, fluid gutter, generous whitespace.

Rail + stage
Stepped rail (active / done / muted) drives a single stage. Used for guided workflows.
Split — two outputs
One source branches into two surfaces (organizer dashboard + participant report).
Bento
Varied tiles for trust / overview grids: one hero, several equal.
Applying it — the product build
Carry forward
  • Tokens are the contract — ship colors, type, radius, shadow as CSS variables / a theme file, not one-off values.
  • Reuse the component set above for every screen; keep surface treatment identical across cards, tables, panels.
  • Every score ships with confidence + source. No bare numbers.
  • Keep the AI→human boundary visible: advisory styling for AI, named human for decisions, an audit line on every action.
  • Light surfaces for the product app; the dark/cinematic register stays for marketing and hero moments.
Keep watch
  • Benchmark and research figures shown in marketing are illustrative — replace with measured data before public claims.
  • Don't let the lens gradient leak into backgrounds or non-signal UI.
  • Don't introduce new accent hues; extend with lightness, not new colors.
  • Privacy is communicated with words and restraint, never security-theater iconography.
  • Native selects / date inputs can't be styled — build custom controls to match this system.

EvalLense09 · Gradient system
09 · Gradient System

One lens, many lights.

The lens gradient is the brand's signal — but it has a small, disciplined family. Linear gradients mark data and the mark; radial glows are atmosphere; the warm gradient is reserved for transitional moments.

Linear — signal & surface
Lens — primary

118° · saturated
Hero numbers, marketing moments

135° · #3A1D8F→#1D8F7E
On dark surfaces, low-glare fills
Ink — mono
160° · #0A0A0D→#1B1B26
Dark panels, statement surfaces
Warm — transition
118° · amber→orange→red
Bridges between sections only
Neutral chrome
180° · #FFF→#FCFCFD
Window chrome, subtle bars
Radial — atmosphere (backgrounds only)
Halo
radial · violet over ink
Behind dark statements & hero
Mist
radial · low-opacity lens on canvas
Section backgrounds, light glow
Do
  • Use linear lens on numbers, the mark, key data.
  • Use radial glows at very low opacity for depth.
  • Switch to deep / ink variants on dark surfaces.
Avoid
  • Lens gradient as a full content background.
  • Warm gradient inside the product UI (transitions only).
  • Stacking multiple bright gradients on one screen.

Do
  • One primary (violet) action per view.
  • Reserve green for confirmed / approved actions.
  • Keep the same button set across light and dark.
Avoid
  • Two violet primaries competing on one screen.
  • Gradient fills on buttons (gradient = signal, not chrome).
  • Drop shadows on dark — use borders instead.
