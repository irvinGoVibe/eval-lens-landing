Here is what is next on the EvalLense roadmap, in order. The next feature is a Hackathon judge. After that, we are building Truth Check. Both do the same job for you: more signal on every submission, with the final decision still yours.

## Next: a judge built for hackathons

Pitch competitions run on slides. Hackathons run on what the team actually built — a repository, a working prototype, a demo video, a live URL. So the next feature reads those, not just the deck.

The Hackathon judge scores execution across six dimensions — demo, technical depth, problem impact, innovation, UX, and delivery readiness — and goes where a pitch judge cannot:

- **It reviews the code.** Repository structure, test coverage, and whether the demo does what the team claims.
- **It watches the demo.** Evidence pulled from the video and the live product, not only the slides.
- **It protects execution.** A polished story cannot outrank a working build. No demo, no top spot.

![](/assets/blog/whats-next/hackathon.png)
:::

For anyone running a hackathon, that turns a pile of repos and demo links into a ranked, evidence-backed review — at the volume a real event throws at you.

## Then: Truth Check

A deck is a sales document. It states a market size, a growth curve, a team's track record, a working demo. Some of it holds up. Some of it does not. Truth Check is the judge that checks before any of it reaches a score.

It reads the factual claims out of a deck and verifies them against outside sources, then returns a clear verdict on each one:

- **Confirmed** — the claim holds up.
- **Disputed** — the evidence says otherwise.
- **Not found** — nothing outside the deck backs it either way.

:::gallery
![](/assets/blog/whats-next/truth-check.png)
:::

Those verdicts land in the report, each traceable to what it was checked against, so you can see which claims to trust, which to question, and which to raise in the live session.

> Truth Check does not reject anyone and does not decide anything. It tells you which numbers earned their place in the pitch.

## Why this order

Hackathons first, because hackathon organizers are asking for it now. Truth Check next, because checking claims well is its own build — and worth doing properly rather than rushing.

Neither changes the part that matters most. AI structures the batch, surfaces the evidence, and checks the claims. You read the report, set the final score, and rank the room. EvalLense is the lens — the decision stays yours.
