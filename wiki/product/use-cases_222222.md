## Роль и аудитория

- **Роль страницы:** дать посетителю **узнать себя** в сегменте и почувствовать,
  что продукт под его сценарий — там, где много деков, мало времени и нужно
  прозрачно выбрать сильнейших.
- **Для кого:** организаторы pitch competition (ICP #1), хакатонов, акселераторы,
  VC-фонды, ангелы, корпоративные инноваторы, грант-программы, университеты.
- **Ключевое сообщение:** один и тот же зажим — **время против объективности** —
  бьёт по всем; EvalLense решает его в *твоём* формате события: один продукт,
  много workflow.
- **Целевое действие:** Book a Demo → `/company/contact` (сегмент-специфичный CTA).

### 1. Hero — `light`

### 2. Общий зажим — `ink`
- **Eyebrow:** The shared problem
- **Heading:** Every program is squeezed between time and fairness
- **Body:** A careful read of one deck takes 20–30 minutes. Across dozens or hundreds, the process stretches into weeks — or gets cut to a skim. And even with time, two honest reviewers can land three points apart on the same deck. On a big batch, speed and fairness suffer at once. EvalLense is built for exactly that batch.

### 3. Найди свой сегмент — `light`
- **Eyebrow:** Find your program
- **Heading:** One system, your workflow
- **Subhead:** Different programs, the same job — turn a batch into a defensible shortlist. Find yours below.
- **Cards (horizontal gallery — сегмент + job-to-be-done; у неякорных — короткий пример-масштаб; у якорных — указатель «in depth below», чтобы не дублировать числа секций 4–5):**
  - **Pitch Competitions** — Score dozens of pitches against one rubric and rank finalists in a day, not a month. *(in depth below)*
  - **Accelerators & Incubators** — Select a cohort consistently — compare founders side by side on the same basis. *(in depth below)*
  - **VC Funds** — Filter early dealflow fast, and walk into the first call with a structured read. *(in depth below)*
  - **Angel Investors** — Get a structured first pass before you spend an evening on diligence. *e.g. 15 decks from a syndicate share.*
  - **Corporate Innovation** — Compare startups on fit, readiness, and evidence — not on who pitched loudest. *e.g. 80 entries to an open-innovation call.*
  - **Grant Programs** — Review applications against fixed criteria, with a record you can defend. *e.g. 120 grant applications.*
  - **Universities** — Run student and research selection on one transparent standard. *e.g. a 50-team demo day.*
  - **Hackathons** — Evaluate many teams quickly, with a leaderboard and questions for the live round. *e.g. a 100-team weekend hack. (Today via the pitch rubric; hackathon-specific mode on the roadmap.)*
- **Bridge line (сигнпост сразу под галереей):** We go deep on three of the most common below — every other program runs the same way.
- *Все example-сценарии на странице — illustrative: гипотетические программы, не клиенты.* (единый дисклеймер, чтобы не вешать метку на каждый пример)

### 4. Pitch Competitions, end to end — `ink`
- **Eyebrow:** Pitch competitions · the flagship case
- **Heading:** A hundred pitches in, a ranked shortlist out
- **Subhead:** The case EvalLense was built for first. Collect the whole field, evaluate it against one rubric, and reach the finals with a ranked, defensible board — each step lights up as you scroll.
- **Steps (pinned, 5):**
  1. **Open submissions** — Share one link; teams upload their own decks into your Entry Hub.
  2. **Run the whole field** — Six judges score every deck against P1–P6, in parallel — overnight if you like, no one watching.
  3. **Read before you meet** — Each contender comes with an evidence-linked report and the questions to ask on stage.
  4. **Rank the board** — A leaderboard sorted by your Final Score, ready to defend to sponsors and co-judges.
  5. **Pick the winners** — You decide, with the evidence in front of you.
- **Example scenario:** A 60-deck regional competition. One link collects every entry; the field is scored overnight; the jury reaches finals day with a ranked board and questions per finalist — instead of ~25 hours of pre-reading.
- **Segment CTA:** Book a demo for your competition → `/company/contact`

### 5. Accelerators & investors — `light`
- **Eyebrow:** Accelerators & investors
- **Heading:** Cohort selection and dealflow, on one basis
- **Subhead:** The same engine, two adjacent jobs — both about reading more, faster, without losing the thread.
- **Split — left (Accelerators & Incubators):**
  - **Select a cohort you can defend** — Score every application against the same bar, compare founders side by side, and back a cohort decision with evidence instead of a reviewer's gut. Reusable for every intake round.
  - *Example scenario:* A 200-application cohort. Every application scored against the same bar; the team defends the shortlist with evidence — reading that would run 80+ hours, done before the committee meets.
- **Split — right (VC & Angel Investors):**
  - **Filter dealflow, then go deep** — Every inbound deck gets a structured first pass, so partners spend their hours on the few worth a call — and walk in already knowing where to press.
  - *Example scenario:* An inbound week of 40 decks. Each gets a structured first pass overnight; partners spend their hours on the 5 worth a call.
- **Segment CTA:** Talk to us about your cohort or dealflow → `/company/contact`

### 6. Общий фундамент — `ink`
- **Eyebrow:** Under every workflow
- **Heading:** Different programs, the same engine
- **Subhead:** Whatever your segment, a run leaves you the same toolkit — that's why one system covers them all.
- **Tiles (compact bento — свёрнутый «что получает каждый», без пересказа Overview):**
  - **Entry Hub** — one intake point, by hand or self-upload link
  - **Six independent judges** — P1–P6, each score with a confidence signal
  - **Evidence-based reports** — scores tied to the deck, with gaps and risks
  - **Leaderboard** — ranked by your human Final Score
  - **Questions for live Q&A** — ready-made, per dimension

### 7. Окупаемость и честность — `light`
- **Eyebrow:** What it's worth
- **Heading:** Reclaim the reading, defend the decision
- **Subhead:** The machine does the reading you don't have time for; you spend your hours on the calls that matter — on one fair standard.
- **Stat-band:**
  - **20–30 min → 0** — human reading per deck: you start at the report, not the raw slides
  - **~40 hours reclaimed** — per 100-deck batch (a full review week)
  - **One rubric** — every entry scored the same way, so comparisons are fair
  - **The final call, always yours** — AI prepares the analysis; a person decides
- **Fairness points (рядом со стат-бандом):**
  - **One standard** — every applicant measured against the same criteria, not the reviewer's mood.
  - **Feedback teams can use** — a structured read, not just a yes or a no.
  - **A record you can defend** — an evidence-linked trail behind every ranking.
- **Label (мелким):** Time figures are illustrative, based on a 20–30 minute manual read per deck.

## Открытые вопросы

- **Якорь сегментов = vision-приоритет.** Глубину дали Pitch Competitions (#1),
  Accelerators, VC/Angels — по `vision.md`. Прежний бриф якорил VC; если GTM-фокус
  сместился, переставить акцент (например, поднять VC выше).
- **Hackathon — сегмент vs режим.** В `scope.md` Hackathon-mode post-MVP, но
  `judges.md`/ADR-012/`PROJECT-ENTRYPOINT` дают Hackathon first-class (J-H1…J-H5 /
  H1–H6). Показан как сегмент с пометкой «сейчас через pitch-rubric»; H1–H6/code-review
  не обещаем до релиза режима.
- **Outcome-числа — illustrative** (оценка фаундера, не measured). Появятся
  сегментные замеры — заменить.
- **Example scenarios — illustrative, не кейсы.** Гипотетические типы программ +
  числа из нашей outcome-математики, под **единым дисклеймером** под галереей (не
  метка на каждом примере). **Без выдуманных брендов, логотипов и цитат** (это была
  бы фабрикация). Появятся реальные клиент-кейсы — заменить на настоящие Case
  Studies (отдельно); дисклеймер держать, пока сценарии гипотетические.
- **De-dup чисел:** у якорных сегментов (competitions/accel/VC) пример живёт только
  в их deep-секции (4–5), в галерее — указатель «in depth below»; у неякорных — в
  галерее. Каждый сегмент = одна конкретная картинка.
