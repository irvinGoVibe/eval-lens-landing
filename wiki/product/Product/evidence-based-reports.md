# Evidence-Based Reports

## Page purpose

Explain the main output of EvalLense: structured reports where scores and conclusions are connected to deck content.

## User task

Help the user understand what they receive after evaluation and why the report is useful for review, shortlist, and decision-making.

## Main idea to communicate

Every score should be explainable. Every important conclusion should be connected to evidence from the pitch deck.

## What the page should avoid

Do not describe this as gap analysis or due diligence. Current product language should stay close to what EvalLense actually produces.

## What the report contains

```text
Judge evaluations
Dimension scores
Evidence references
Strengths and weaknesses
Final summaries
Exportable reports
```

## Section structure

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

```text
Position reports as explainable outputs, not black-box AI scores.
```

Message:

```text
Get structured reports that explain how each startup was evaluated and what evidence supports the result.
```

### 2. Why normal AI scoring is not enough

Purpose:

```text
Create the need for evidence-based evaluation.
```

Pain points:

```text
Scores without reasoning are hard to trust
Review teams need to understand why a deck ranked high or low
Founders may need structured feedback
Program owners need a decision trail
```

### 3. Report components

Purpose:

```text
Show exactly what the user receives.
```

Content:

```text
Dimension scores - structured scores by evaluation dimension
Judge assessments - reasoning from each judge lens
Evidence references - where the reasoning comes from in the deck
Strengths - what the deck communicates well
Weaknesses - what appears weak, unclear, or unsupported
Final summary - concise decision-support summary
Exportable report - shareable output for internal review
```
